I am a PhD student in Astrophysics at Arizona State University's School of Earth and Space Exploration. My research focuses on extragalactic astrophysics, computational astrophysics, and the diffuse gas surrounding galaxies, especially the circumgalactic and intragroup medium.

I use large-scale simulations, synthetic spectroscopy, observational comparisons, and statistical tools to study how galaxies evolve and interact with their environments. I am also interested in machine learning, simulation-based inference, image processing, and scientific software development for astronomy.

Before ASU, I completed a B.Tech. in Engineering Physics at the Indian Institute of Technology Delhi in July 2023 and worked as a visiting student at the Raman Research Institute in Bangalore from May to July 2022.

Research

Diffuse gas, feedback, and the baryon cycle.

My current work connects simulations and observables to understand how galaxies exchange matter and energy with their surroundings.

Circumgalactic and Intragroup Medium

Studying the diffuse gas around galaxies and galaxy groups to understand how baryons cycle through galaxies and their environments.

Cosmological Simulations

Using large-scale simulations, including IllustrisTNG and SIMBA, to connect galaxy formation physics, feedback, and gas structure.

Synthetic Spectroscopy

Generating mock quasar absorption spectra and forward-modeled observables for direct comparison with telescope data.

Data-Driven Astrophysics

Developing computational and statistical tools, including machine learning and simulation-based inference, for astronomical datasets.
